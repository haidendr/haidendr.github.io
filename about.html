About Me

I am a versatile Data Analyst based in Portland, Oregon. My background includes a Master’s in Data Analytics and a Bachelor’s in Management Information Systems. I bring together business insight and technical expertise to transform raw data into meaningful outcomes.

My professional experience began in the automotive aftermarket sector, where I focused on reporting, data cleanup, and validation using SQL, Microsoft Access, FoxPro, and other legacy systems. These early roles gave me a strong foundation in practical analytics and data integrity.

Since then, I have built my expertise through academic work that spans financial modeling, healthcare-related research, public policy analytics, and data architecture. Some of my projects have explored equity-focused immunization trends, examined the effects of tariff policies on market behavior, and involved designing OLTP-based data warehouse systems with dimensional models and star schemas.

I am passionate about the stories data can reveal, whether I am analyzing trends in portfolio performance, exploring real estate metrics, or uncovering patterns in policy datasets. I approach every analysis with curiosity, precision, and a commitment to clarity.

Beyond the realm of data, I am a parent to two children, including one who is on the autism spectrum. These personal experiences shape my approach to inclusive and thoughtful problem-solving. In my spare time, I enjoy diving into new statistical methods, programming tools, and innovations in data science that help me continue growing as a professional.
